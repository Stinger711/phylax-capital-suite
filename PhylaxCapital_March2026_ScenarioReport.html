What Happened in March 2026
Geopolitical trigger: Over the last weekend of February 2026, US-Israeli forces launched strikes in Iran, killing the Supreme Leader. The resulting Middle East crisis drove a global risk-off episode. Oil prices surged, emerging market funds suffered heavy outflows, the South African Rand weakened sharply against the USD, and the JSE Top 40 entered a steep correction despite South Africa's resource-heavy index initially being partially insulated by rising gold prices. By the end of March, the ALSI had fallen 12.97% — one of the sharpest single-month corrections in recent years.

Why the Phylax return of +32.3% is credible in this scenario: The fund purchased put options deeply out of the money (10% below the index). When the ALSI fell 12.97%, the puts moved from deeply OTM to 3,550 points in the money. The delta-gamma-vega effect means the payout is non-linear — a 13% index fall translates to a large multiple on the small premium paid. This is exactly how tail-risk protection is designed to work: inexpensive to hold (funded by yield), explosive when needed. The 23.7× protection-to-cost ratio confirms the structural logic.
