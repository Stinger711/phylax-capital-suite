01 — The Problem

Sharp Drawdowns Are Not Rare — They Are Scheduled

Analysis of 20 years of JSE All Share monthly return data (2005–2024, 240 months) reveals a pattern that most investors underestimate: large, sudden drawdowns occur with far greater frequency than traditional risk models suggest.

20-Year ALSI Crash Frequency

15 rolling 3-month windows exceeded a 10% decline — one every 16 months on average. Six distinct crash events in 20 years gives an average gap of 3.3 years between major events. An equity portfolio manager on a 5-year mandate will statistically encounter at least two qualifying crash periods.

The critical insight is not just frequency — it is timing. The COVID-19 crash arrived when the SAVI implied volatility index was at a multi-year low of 16.5%. Markets had priced almost zero tail risk. By the time volatility spiked above 50%, buying the same protection had become four times more expensive and the crash had already occurred. Waiting for a signal to hedge is not a strategy — it is a guarantee of buying protection at exactly the wrong time.

02 — The Solution

Permanent, Unconditional Protection With No Timing Risk

Macro Protector is structured as a permanent 3–7% carve-out from an equity portfolio allocation. A small, systematically managed sleeve that converts crash events into capital windfalls while protecting the rest of the portfolio — a strategy well-established among sophisticated institutional allocators globally and now available within the South African RIHF regulatory framework.

What It Is

  • Structural capital floor: worst case is 0% nominal (before fees) — the JIBAR interest earned in advance funds the option premiums, so the principal is always intact
  • Asymmetric payoff: modest cost in quiet markets, multiples of cost in crashes
  • Continuous rolling ladder: no timing decisions — overlapping tranches ensure protection is always in place
  • Exchange traded: SAFEX-listed ALSI options, centrally cleared

What It Is Not

  • Not a market hedge: gradual bear markets may not trigger payouts
  • Not a volatility fund: it buys protection, not volatility exposure
  • Not leveraged: principal cannot be impaired — only the option budget (JIBAR interest) is at risk
  • Not discretionary: no tactical positioning or market timing

The core principle — maintaining constant, low-cost protection funded entirely by yield rather than capital — has delivered compelling results for global institutional allocators during every major market dislocation of the past two decades. Macro Protector delivers this structural advantage within the South African regulatory framework.

03 — How It Works

The Rolling Ladder Architecture

Rather than a single quarterly position, Macro Protector operates a continuous rolling ladder of overlapping put tranches across multiple expiry dates. At any given time the fund holds several live option positions at different stages of their life cycle — providing constant, uninterrupted coverage regardless of when a crash occurs within the calendar.

The Capital Floor Mechanics

Each tranche sizes a JIBAR-linked cash position to mature at exactly NAV at its expiry date. The interest earned in advance on that cash — not the principal — funds the purchase of out-of-the-money put options. The principal is structurally ring-fenced: the option premium costs nothing beyond foregone yield.

Rolling Before the Theta Cliff

Positions are not held to expiry. Each tranche is sold before the theta cliff — the point where daily time-value decay accelerates sharply. For 90-day puts this typically occurs at 40–50 days before expiry. The rolling ladder means a fresh tranche is always being entered while an existing tranche is being rolled, maintaining continuous coverage at every point in time.

  • Buy: puts with 90 days to the next SAFEX expiry, 10% OTM, funded by JIBAR interest
  • Monitor: daily pricing and delta tracking across all live tranches
  • Roll: sell before theta cliff, capturing both intrinsic value and elevated time value in a crash
  • Replace: enter fresh puts for the next expiry, resetting the clock and rebuilding coverage

This ladder structure means there is no single quarterly window in which a crash must occur to generate a payout. Every trading day the fund holds live protection. A crash at any point in the month triggers the appropriate live tranche — the overlapping architecture eliminates the single-expiry timing risk entirely.

T+1 Daily Liquidity

The rolling ladder structure combined with SAFEX's daily trading ensures the fund maintains T+1 liquidity at all times. There are no lock-up periods, no quarterly dealing windows, and no notice periods beyond same-day instruction. Redemption proceeds are settled on the next business day.

04 — The Mathematics

Black-Scholes Pricing — Buy Cheap, Sell Expensive

All option pricing uses the industry-standard Black-Scholes model for equity index options. The put option price at entry is determined by the current ALSI level, the 10% OTM strike, JIBAR as the risk-free rate, and the prevailing SAVI implied volatility. The critical insight is the asymmetry between entry vol and crash vol.

How the Capital Floor Works

Each tranche of the fund holds a JIBAR-linked cash position sized to mature at exactly the amount invested. The interest earned in advance on that cash — not the principal — is used to purchase out-of-the-money put options. If the options expire worthless, the cash matures back to the full amount invested. The principal is never at risk. The only cost is the foregone interest on the option budget — a small fraction of the total allocation.

The Vol Asymmetry — The Engine of the Strategy

Options are purchased continuously when markets are calm and implied vol is low. When a crash occurs, volatility spikes dramatically — increasing the value of every contract held far beyond its intrinsic value alone. The fund systematically buys at low vol and sells at high vol, without needing to time any market entry. A −33% crash typically causes SAVI to spike from ~18% to ~65% — the same contract that cost R4,500 at entry becomes worth over R110,000 at the roll date. That is the compounding of intrinsic value and vol premium working simultaneously.

The COVID Lesson — The Ladder Buys When It Is Cheap

In Q1 2020 implied volatility was at a multi-year low of 16.5%. Had our option budget been deployed at that level — R409,284 per tranche — it would have funded puts that paid out R35.2m per tranche at the roll date. If that same allocation had instead only entered in March 2020 after volatility spiked above 50%, the identical budget would have purchased fewer than 30 contracts. The ladder's continuous, unconditional rolling is the mechanism that ensures positioning always occurs at the cheapest available vol — never scrambling to buy after the crash has already started.

The 2022 Lesson — What Insurance Costs

In 2022 the ALSI fell 8.9%, ending above the 10% OTM strike — options would have expired worthless. The hypothetical fund return would have been −1.1%, with the total cost being R274k in foregone JIBAR yield on the option budget. That is the total and complete cost of a full year of portfolio protection. As a percentage of a R500m equity portfolio: 0.055%. The 5% carve-out itself would not have been in equities during the drawdown — it would not have lost equity value. This is the asymmetric cost structure that makes the strategy defensible regardless of whether a qualifying crash occurs.

Risk Considerations

Options Expire OTM

If the ALSI does not breach the 10% OTM strike at roll date, options have no intrinsic value. The fund returns the capital floor — 0% nominal before fees. This is the expected outcome in most years and is fully anticipated in the cost structure.

SAFEX Execution at Smaller Fund Size

Bid/offer spreads on OTM ALSI puts are wider at smaller contract counts. Below critical mass, execution costs are higher as a percentage of premium. Once the fund reaches institutional scale, tighter spreads and better pricing are achievable — this risk diminishes as AUM grows.

Structural Advantages of the Rolling Ladder

Intra-Quarter Coverage — Eliminated as a Risk

With overlapping tranches at multiple expiry dates, a crash at any point in the calendar hits at least one live position. There is no single quarterly window that must be "hit" to generate a payout. The ladder converts timing risk into a structural advantage.

High Entry Vol — No Longer a Risk

Because the ladder rolls continuously, individual tranches are entered at various vol levels over time. High vol on one entry date is offset by lower vol on others — the portfolio cost averages across many entry points. No single entry decision determines the overall cost or coverage.

Vol Spike at Roll — A Feature, Not a Risk

When a crash occurs, implied volatility spikes dramatically. Because positions are rolled (sold) rather than held to expiry, this vol spike is captured in the sale price. The ladder is specifically designed to sell into elevated vol — this is where the majority of the payout premium above intrinsic value is generated.

Theta Management — Pre-Cliff Roll

All positions are rolled before the theta cliff — the accelerated decay zone at the final 40–50 days before expiry. Pre-cliff rolling preserves time value and ensures each tranche is replaced while it still carries meaningful premium, maintaining the coverage quality of the ladder at all times.
