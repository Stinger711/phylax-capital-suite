⚠ Black Swans Are Not Rare — They Are Scheduled

The term "black swan" implies rarity. The data tells a different story. In the 20 years from 2005 to 2024, the JSE All Share Index experienced 15 distinct rolling 3-month windows where it declined more than 10%. That is not one-in-a-generation risk — it is a recurring feature of equity markets that arrives, on average, every 16 months.

The events that cause these drawdowns — geopolitical shocks, credit crises, pandemics, political disruption — are individually unpredictable but collectively inevitable. A DFM managing a 5-year mandate can expect to live through at least two of them. The question is not whether a large drawdown will occur — it is whether your portfolio is structured to survive it, and ideally, to benefit from it.

Macro Protector is sized precisely for this reality: a permanent 5% carve-out that costs the portfolio less than 0.15% per annum in foregone yield, and returns multiples of the portfolio’s equity loss in the precise quarters when that protection is most needed.

Key Insight
A drawdown exceeding 10% in a rolling quarter occurs roughly once every 16 months on average. In a 5-year mandate, a DFM can statistically expect 3–4 such windows. In at least one of them, the ALSI will fall more than 20%.
The Case for Permanent Protection
The Timing Problem
No DFM can consistently predict which quarter a crash arrives. The 2020 COVID crash struck when the SAVI was at a multi-year low of 16% — markets were pricing zero tail risk. By the time vol spiked, puts were 4× more expensive. Systematic rolling removes the timing problem.
The Cost Argument
In a year with no crash, the maximum cost of Macro Protector is the JIBAR yield on the option budget — typically <0.15% of the total portfolio. The 5% carve-out also foregoes some upside in bull markets, but the capital guarantee ensures zero drawdown on the sleeve itself.
The Return Profile
Across 6 distinct crash events in 20 years, a hypothetical 5% Phylax allocation would have returned between +25% and +139% on the sleeve in the crash quarter, saving between R4m and R44m on a R500m portfolio per event. The protection-to-cost ratio averaged over 35×.
